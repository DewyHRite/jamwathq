What is the J-1 program?

The J-1 (BridgeUSA) program is a State Department-sponsored summer work and travel program known as the J-1 program. This program is for Jamaican university students who want to work abroad and experience American culture during summer break. Participants work temporarily in hospitality or tourism and exchange cultures.

Who can apply?

The J1 Exchange Visitor Program offers Jamaican students valuable opportunities to gain professional experience through internships and summer work. Eligible participants include full-time university students who are currently pursuing a 4-year BSc degree and maintain a minimum GPA of 2.0. The Summer Work and Travel category is especially suitable for Jamaican students enrolled in accredited colleges or universities. Additional eligibility criteria encompass relevant academic or professional experience. Applicants must secure a placement with an approved sponsor and fulfill requirements such as English language proficiency, valid health insurance, and appropriate documentation.

J-1 Program Overview

Local Agencies

J1 program agencies are organizations that sponsor international students, teachers, researchers, and other foreign nationals to participate in exchange programs in the United States. These agencies facilitate the process of obtaining J-1 visas, which allow individuals to work, study, or teach in the U.S. for a limited time.

Sponsors

In the J-1 program, the designated sponsors are the agencies responsible for connecting students (exchange visitors) to host employers. These sponsors are approved by the U.S. Department of State. They ensure compliance with regulations, facilitate placements, and provide support throughout the exchange experience.

Host Employers

J-1 host employers in the U.S. range from small enterprises to large firms, nonprofits, educational institutions, and government offices. These employers work with designated sponsors to ensure J-1 program compliance and meaningful cultural exchanges. J-1 host employers provide internships, training, and professional development while promoting community service and cultural exchange.

Summary

J-1 (BrigdeUSA) Program

The J-1 Exchange Visitor Program is a U.S. program for nonimmigrant visas that promotes international education and culture. It welcomes international participants to the US for professional training, academic study, teaching, research, or cultural activities. The State Department authorizes a network of public and private entities as program sponsors. The J-1 visa has many categories for diverse participant characteristics and goals. Intern/trainee, student/secondary school, teacher, professor, researcher, specialist, and cultural programs like Au Pair, Camp Counselor, and Summer Work Travel are common. Each category is meant to help participants enter the U.S. in a way that fosters personal growth and cultural interchange. The sponsor's DS-2019 form (Certificate of Eligibility) is essential, confirming the participant's program, stay, and J-1 visa eligibility. Employers and schools provide a practical environment for participants to work, study, and experience U.S. workplace culture. Cultural interchange is a key component of the J-1 program, encouraging participants to learn about American culture and share their own. SEVIS tracks J-1 participants' status, compliance, and movements during their stay to ensure the program meets its goals and host organizations meet regulatory requirements.
