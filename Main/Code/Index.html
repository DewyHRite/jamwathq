What is JamWatHQ?

Welcome to JamWatHQ, your one-stop hub for reliable information tailored to Jamaican J1 students. We're here to help you confidently navigate your work and travel journey.

At Jamwathq, you can explore a wealth of carefully curated resources:

  • Agency Information: Find a comprehensive list of approved agencies and sponsors along with agency rankings to help you choose trusted partners on your journey.

  • Latest News & Alerts: Stay informed with new articles related to the J1 program, along with up-to-date scam alerts and awareness notifications to keep you safe.

  • Embassy Resources: Access essential embassy links that include the visa application website, I-94 details, and the DHS I-901 website, ensuring you have all the official tools at your fingertips.

  • Practical Guidance: Dive into our FAQs, explore resources on general work and travel information, and learn which jobs to avoid to make informed decisions every step of the way.

Our goal is to deliver clear, no-nonsense guidance so that you can navigate your J1 experience with confidence, safety, and a deep connection to your Jamaican heritage. With Jamwathq, you’re not just informed—you’re empowered to take on the world.

Who we are not!

At Jamwathq, we want to be clear that we are solely an information hub for the J1 program. We are not an agency, a sponsor, a recruiter, or a host employer, nor do we serve as a representative or consultant of any kind. We also do not operate a WhatsApp group or any similar channel. Our purpose is to provide reliable and straightforward information to help you safely navigate the complexities of the J1 experience. If you encounter anyone claiming to be an official Jamwathq agent, please report that individual to the police immediately.

What is the J1 program?

The J-1 (BridgeUSA) program is a State Department-sponsored summer work and travel program known as the J-1 program. This program is for Jamaican university students who want to work abroad and experience American culture during summer break. Participants work temporarily in hospitality or tourism and exchange cultures.
